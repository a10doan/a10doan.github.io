About Me

I had always considered myself to be a "thinker", constantly experimenting and making sense of data, which lead me to the pharmaceutical field because it requires high degree of quantitative skill, deductive reasoning, and meticulous attention to detail. After several years as a pharmaceutical research associate, I did some soul searching and realized how much I enjoyed studying Economics during my undergraduate studies and that I found myself fascinated by Economic data. It was then I went back to school in pursuit of a graduate degree in Economics and a certificate in Data Analytics. A successful Data Analyst should have a strong foundation in both backgrounds in order to formulate questions and draw conclusions from data.
